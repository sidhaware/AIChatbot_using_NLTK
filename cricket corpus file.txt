Cricket is a popular sport played worldwide. The game is played between two teams, each consisting of eleven players. Matches are typically held on a circular field called a cricket ground.

Basic Cricket Facts:
1. Cricket has three main formats: Test cricket, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 (T20) matches.
2. The governing body for international cricket is the International Cricket Council (ICC).
3. The cricket ball is made of leather and weighs between 155.9 and 163.0 grams.
4. Batsmen score runs by hitting the ball and running between wickets.

Cricket Statistics:
1. Sachin Tendulkar holds the record for the highest number of runs in international cricket.
2. Sir Don Bradman has the highest batting average in Test cricket.
3. Muttiah Muralitharan is the highest wicket-taker in international cricket.
4. The highest team score in ODIs is 481/6 by England against Australia.
5. The fastest century in T20 Internationals was scored by David Miller in 35 balls.

Cricket Teams:
1. The Indian cricket team is known as the "Men in Blue."
2. Australia's team is nicknamed the "Baggy Greens."
3. England's team is often referred to as the "Three Lions."

Cricket World Cups:
1. The ICC Cricket World Cup is held every four years.
2. The West Indies won the first two Cricket World Cups in 1975 and 1979.
3. India won the World Cup in 1983, led by captain Kapil Dev.
